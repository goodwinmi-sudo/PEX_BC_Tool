 Indemnity Caps, Escalations and Best Practices
Indemnity exposes Google to legal and financial risk and can arise late in the negotiation cycle   
Aim to always negotiate to our standards and not offer out-sized indemnity to partners
  
Guidelines to consider when evaluating indemnity caps:
Include in deal costs,
 and cap to a financially reasonable amount (the PA bears the cost)
Google should not offer significant capped (e.g. >10M) or 
uncapped
 indemnities 
outside of approved standard positions
 without obtaining approvals
Google should not offer indemnity for free Google products.
Include indemnity with other deal terms early in negotiations 
Approvals are required as follows 
(refer to

apped >$10M or uncapped

Questions to evaluate as you work with Legal, Finance, and Product to evaluate indemnity caps:
What is the significance of the deal / deal value / competitive pressures?
Is the indemnity controllable or uncontrollable by Google?  Is the indemnity mutual (both ways)?
Per Legal, what are common indemnity payout amounts, claims, or litigation costs we are seeing?
For IP indemnity, types of claims that are typical with this type of product? 
For Regulatory indemnity, how active is the policy environment and what are the risks?
Is there any precedent / spillover to other deals with the same partner or other partners?

Side Agreements & Retrodating 
Side agreements are a type of policy violation, where written or verbal agreements are not authorized in accordance with Alphabet’s Commitment and Signature Authority Policy, and that are outside of and/or supersede any terms and conditions of the original contract.
Side agreements present a risk of financial reporting. 
Side agreements can include arrangements where promises are made without advance approval for:
Performance of Google's services
Spend commitments
Commercial terms
Promised credits or refunds if a Google service fails to meet specified customer or partner expectations
